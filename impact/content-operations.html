Content Operations

Centralized CMS Digital Forms & E-Signature Product Catalog Training & Update Distribution Single Source of Truth

Context
Ooredoo's SMB sales force — 50+ direct field reps, 100+ channel partner agents, and retail staff — selling a 30+ product portfolio across Mobile, Fixed, and ICT. Every customer conversation depended on having the right collateral, the right pricing, and the right form, in the right version, at the moment of truth.

Built a PLM-integrated content engine to centralize all sales and technical assets. This system serves as the definitive Single Source of Truth, ensuring that governed messaging, presentations, pricing, order forms, promos, sales plays, and recorded training sessions are delivered in real-time to the sales field, effectively eliminating information silos, accelerating the Lead-to-Cash cycle, and uplifting customer satisfaction.

1

Centralized Sales CMS — Single Source of Truth

Sales materials were scattered across personal folders and shared drives. Teams routinely worked from outdated pricing, stale presentations, and unofficial product specs — undermining both credibility and conversion in front of the customer.

Sourced, configured, and deployed a centralized Content Management Platform as the single source of truth for every customer-facing and field-facing artifact — customer sales presentations, technical solution presentations, product specs, pricing sheets, promotions, training videos, competitive teardowns, and order forms.

The CMS also housed the asset libraries that powered all Sales Plays across the SMB portfolio — battlecards, talk tracks, discovery banks, ROI calculators, email sequences, and methodology playbooks (MEDDPICC, Challenger, Command of the Message) — tagged by Play, segment, product, and motion for sellers to retrieve in-flow.

Content Management System

Established a governance process with Base Management and Product Managers so content was reviewed, versioned, and refreshed on a defined cadence — every approved asset traceable to an owner and a current version. Drove adoption across the full sales team through structured onboarding sessions and active enforcement until legacy folders went dark.

Embedded in PLM. The CMS sat inside the company's Product Lifecycle Management (PLM) process — content readiness (presentations, pricing, battlecards, order forms, training assets) was a gated step in every product launch, not an afterthought. Field reps received fully populated content kits the moment a product went live.

2

Digital Order Forms & E-Signature

Order forms were paper-based, dated and with multiple variants per product line. Errors, missing signatures, and re-keying delays added days to time-to-cash.

Redesigned every order form into a structured digital experience with guided fields, built-in validation, and conditional logic that surfaced only the relevant options for the chosen product and bundle.

Integrated e-signature so customers could quote, agree, and sign on the spot — with submissions flowing directly into CRM and Order Management for fulfillment, eliminating paper handling and re-keying entirely.

Order forms were integrated into the PLM process — every product launch reached the field with order-ready forms validated, signed-off, and CRM-routable from day one.

3

Interactive Product Catalog

A 30+ product SMB portfolio organized by technology category overwhelmed customers and left reps without a coherent way to discover and position offerings against actual business needs.

Designed an interactive product and services catalog organized around the six customer-centric need areas, with outcome-led messaging, comparison views, and clear pricing. Deployed the catalog in three places: on retail interactive screens for self-serve walk-in browsing, on field tablets for rep-led discussions in the customer's office, and inside the digital self-service B2B app for online product discovery.

The catalog became the single visual artifact every channel sold from — eliminating version-mismatch between retail, field, and digital, and giving customers a consistent product experience regardless of where the conversation started.

Interactive product catalog

4

Connected Tablet Distribution

A centralized CMS is only useful if it reaches the field. Reps without real-time access to current content end up improvising — the very problem the CMS was meant to solve.

Equipped every field representative with a rugged, LTE-connected tablet pre-loaded with the CMS and the digital order form. Content updates pushed from the CMS were available to every rep in real time — when pricing, promotions, or specs changed, no rep was selling from a stale version twenty minutes later. The tablet also enabled on-the-spot quoting, e-signature, and order submission, turning every customer visit into a fully transactable interaction.

5

Training & Continuous Update Materials

Static collateral isn't enough. Reps need ongoing training, product update videos, and competitive briefings — available on demand, not just in scheduled classroom sessions.

Extended the CMS into a continuous-learning surface: recorded Sales Academy training sessions, weekly product update videos, regulatory tariff briefings, competitive teardowns, and MCQ assessments — all published into the same governed library. Reps could review content on demand, new hires could catch up after the live cohort had moved on, and managers could assign targeted modules in response to performance gaps. The CMS effectively doubled as the on-demand arm of the B2B Sales Academy.

Outcomes
  • Eliminated content silos and version errors across the sales force
  • Accelerated the Lead-to-Cash cycle with always-current collateral and pricing
  • Improved customer experience and conversion through consistent messaging across every channel
  • Established a single source of truth feeding sales, partner, retail, and digital channels
  • Built governed, scalable content infrastructure that compresses time-to-launch for every future product
