Channel Partners — Partner-Led Distribution

SOHO Indirect Channel Partner Enablement Quota Cascade & Commissions Channel Conflict Resolution Sales Plays

Recruited and enabled 5 boutique partner firms with 100+ frontline agents to deliver Sales and Retention to the SOHO segment of Ooredoo's SMB business — equipped with the same tools, content, and certification as direct sellers, so partners could acquire, onboard, and retain customers autonomously without degrading customer experience.

Context
The SOHO segment (single-owner, sub-10-employee businesses) was too granular and geographically dispersed for direct field reps to cover economically — but too high-volume and high-touch to leave to digital alone. A boutique-partner channel was the only way to acquire and retain at scale without inflating CAC.
Proposition
Recruit specialist boutique sales firms with established SOHO relationships, equip them with the same enablement stack as direct reps, and design commercial terms that align partner incentives with Ooredoo's NRR and ARPA goals — not just acquisition volume.
Partner Recruiting & Selection: Identified and recruited 5 boutique sales firms with proven SOHO segment expertise and established customer relationships. Negotiated tiered commercial agreements aligning partner economics with Ooredoo's growth and retention targets, not just acquisition volume.
Channel Conflict Resolution & Coverage Rules: Designed zone-based account ownership rules and a deal-registration protocol to prevent direct-vs-indirect overlap on the same accounts. Codified no-double-coverage clauses in partner contracts and built escalation paths for territory disputes — protecting both direct-rep commissions and partner economics, and keeping the customer experience clean across the channel mix.
Partner Org Design & Operational Independence: Worked with each partner firm to design their internal operating structure — dedicated units for telemarketing, back-office support, and field sales, with team-level roles (sales coaches, team leads, reps) mirroring Ooredoo's own organization. Partners delivered both acquisition and base retention independently — handling escalations, upgrades, and renewals directly — while Ooredoo provided supervisory tooling and performance dashboards. Retention save-ratio matched direct-rep benchmarks; CX held steady. For partners running their own retail shops, equipped shop staff with secure CRM connectivity and full lead-capture integration so partner-shop walk-ins entered the same pipeline as every other channel.
Partner Quota Engineering & Cascade: As part of carrying the SMB division quota, engineered each partner firm's annual quota based on territory potential, agent count, and product mix — then guided partner leadership on how to cascade it to quarterly targets, weekly run-rates, and per-agent commitments. Provided the templates and dashboards partners used to track against the cascade, giving Ooredoo agent-level forecasting resolution within the indirect channel.
Partner Commissions Architecture & Operations: Owned the end-to-end commissions function for the partner channel — designed the comp plans (quotas, ramps, accelerators, clawbacks, SPIFFs, OTE) and authored the partner contracts that codified them; selected and rolled out the commissions system; ran monthly payouts with fraud and verification controls; governed crediting rules and dispute resolution as the single source of truth; and produced attainment, plan-performance, and cost-of-sales reporting. Partnered with Finance on accruals and forecasting and with People on partner-role design.
Partner Enablement at Direct-Rep Parity: Equipped every partner agent with the same stack as direct field reps — rugged LTE-connected tablets, mobile CRM, the PLM-governed CMS with current pricing and battlecards, the interactive product catalog for in-customer discovery, and digital order forms with e-signature.
Extended the B2B Sales Academy to partner agents on the same Sales Associate / Sales Expert certification path as internal sellers, with certification gating access to complex deals and tier-2 commercial terms. For partners running their own retail shops, the catalog also ran on in-shop interactive screens for self-serve customer browsing — partners sold on Ooredoo's standard, from the same single source of truth.

CMS in Field Tablet

Channel Sales Plays & Commercial Bundles: Built role-specific co-sell Sales Plays for partner agents — target cohorts, value hypotheses, discovery scripts, objection libraries, competitive positioning, and pre-configured commercial bundles tuned to SOHO buying patterns. Each Play activated a specific motion (new SMB acquisition, upgrade conversations, VAS cross-sell, renewal saves) so partner agents could quote and close without escalation, without giving up margin or unit economics.
Outcomes
  • Scaled distribution into a previously uneconomical SOHO segment
  • Sustained 5% YoY growth and 104% NRR in a saturated, price-pressured market
  • Built a partner-led acquisition + retention engine independent of direct-sales headcount
  • Established territorial coverage rules that protected both direct-rep commissions and partner economics
  • Equipped partners at direct-rep parity on tools, content, and certification — ensuring consistent customer experience across the channel mix
