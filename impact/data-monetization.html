Data/API Monetization & Usage-Based Revenue

API Productization Usage-Based Monetization Marketing Campaign as a Service Self-Service Platform Regulated Market

Led end-to-end development of Ooredoo's first data monetization product from business case and regulatory clearance through API design, engineering delivery, and commercial launch, generating $100K in first-month revenue, exceeding forecast by 35%.

Context
Ooredoo is a fast-paced multi-national corporation offering telecom, cloud, and ICT services in 13 countries with 100M subscribers. Ooredoo Qatar is the head office operating in adherence with strict national consumer data privacy laws.
Problem
Ooredoo sits on a vast, untapped strategic asset in its consumer datasets. Meanwhile, SMBs were churning out of the Bulk SMS service due to a lack of customer data needed to run targeted campaigns — a missed revenue opportunity for Ooredoo on both sides.
Proposition
Unlock new revenue streams bridging both problems, by building a self-serve Marketing Campaigns as a Service platform allowing SMBs to launch demographic and location-based SMS and WhatsApp campaigns, powered by Ooredoo's anonymized customer data without compromising subscriber privacy.

Business Case & Pricing Model: Developed a Business Case grounded in market analysis, opportunity cost, regulatory constraints, and MVP scope. Designed a pay-per-message pricing model with a premium for advanced targeting — aligning price directly to value delivered — and secured executive approval to fund the initiative.
Product Positioning: Reframed the offering from a commoditized "Bulk SMS" service to a value-added "Marketing Campaigns as a Service" — developing the pricing rationale, sales narrative, and training for Sales and Customer Support teams to position the product effectively at launch.
Regulatory and Privacy Clearance: This was the make-or-break risk for the product. Led the analysis with Legal and Regulatory teams, mapped compliance options with their trade-offs, and presented a recommended path to executives — securing formal approval that set a precedent for all future data monetization initiatives at Ooredoo.
Consent Infrastructure: Designed and drove implementation of consent capture across high-traffic touchpoints — service order forms, retail digital signing screens, and a base-wide consent SMS campaign — alongside new opt-out controls in the consumer mobile app.
Audience Targeting Architecture: Architected a parameterized audience query API giving SMBs self-serve access to Ooredoo's subscriber data without exposing raw demographics — designed to add new targeting dimensions without re-engineering the core flow. Built privacy guardrails including consent-flag filtering, frequency capping, and automatic audience redistribution to protect subscriber experience and the long-term viability of the revenue stream.
Solution Design: Integrated five platforms (CRM, data warehouse, Bulk SMS gateway, Azure Communications for WhatsApp, and payment gateway) behind a clean five-step self-serve flow: select audience → write message → choose channel → pay → launch.

Spend Control Feature: Introduced a Campaign Spend Control feature letting SMBs dynamically resize their target audience to match their budget — directly addressing a key adoption barrier.

Market Awareness: Partnered with Marketing to design and execute a multi-channel awareness campaign — social media, email, SMS, and WhatsApp — educating SMB customers on the new offering with messages tailored to specific verticals (retail, hospitality, healthcare).
Launch & Results: The first month delivered overwhelming validation. More than 50 SMB customers signed up within 30 days, 20% of whom launched campaigns more than once — generating $100K in revenue and exceeding the Business Case 30-day forecast by 35%.
Outcomes
  • Launched the first data monetization product at Ooredoo
  • Generated $100K in first-month revenue, 35% above forecast
  • Solved two commercial problems at once: SMB churn from Bulk SMS and the company’s long-stalled ambition to monetize dormant subscriber data
  • Established the regulatory, consent, and architectural precedent for every future data monetization initiative
  • Built a productized, scalable revenue engine from a previously dormant strategic asset
