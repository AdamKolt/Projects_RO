Lead-to-Cash Optimization

Quote-to-Cash Automation Compressing Deal Velocity Order Accuracy CPQ → Provisioning → Billing → CS Handoff

Ooredoo Qatar's SMB sales operation spans 100+ indirect channel partner sales agents, digital channels, and an outbound telemarketing team — each operating as a largely independent motion with limited data sharing, inconsistent tooling, and fragmented order workflows.

The Lead-to-Cash pipeline had multiple friction points suppressing revenue and inflating costs: leads were identified reactively, agents lacked the data and tools to engage effectively, orders moved through manual paper-based processes, and leadership had limited visibility into performance. I led five concurrent initiatives to systematically eliminate each bottleneck — from first customer contact through to cash collection and executive reporting.

1

SMB Portfolio Restructure — Needs-Based Architecture

The SMB portfolio spanned 30+ products organised by technology type — creating market confusion and leaving sales reps without a coherent way to position offerings against customer needs. Buyers were overwhelmed; reps were underselling.

Conducted a full portfolio audit combining customer journey analysis, sales conversion data, and competitive benchmarking to identify the real decision drivers for SMB buyers. The findings revealed that customers did not think in technology categories — they thought in business outcomes.

Redesigned the go-to-market architecture around six customer-centric need areas, each with outcome-led messaging validated through customer beta testing. The new framework resonated immediately — evidenced by rapid adoption by sales teams and measurable improvements in product discoverability and conversion rates.


The restructured taxonomy was adopted as the standard across all channels — the B2B App, marketing collateral, sales toolkits, and digital self-service — creating a unified GTM message that spoke the customer's language and directly enabled the self-service product discovery experience built into the digital platform.

2

Order Digitization & Cycle Time Reduction

Non-standardized, incomplete paper order forms were causing downstream operational errors, fulfillment delays, and a poor customer experience — with the order signing cycle routinely stretching to more than 48 hours.

Partnered with Product, Operations, and Marketing UX/UI teams to redesign and standardize all order forms into clean, up-to-date fillable PDF documents and digital forms. Centralized them in the sales repository for online completion, download, or direct sharing with customers. Established a digital signing workflow enabling authorized customer signatories to execute orders remotely — compressing the signing step from 48 hours to under one hour. This was the critical unlock in a broader process redesign that cut the total order-to-activation cycle from 9 days to 3.

3

Data-Driven Outbound Engine

The call center was operating reactively — agents had no targeted prospect lists, no structured scripts, and no mechanism to hand off warm leads to field teams. Outreach was cold, conversion was low, and qualified opportunities were routinely lost.

Mined the customer database to identify high-propensity segments based on usage patterns, tenure, and product heat-map coverage gaps. Generated targeted outbound calling lists enriched with account context, giving calling agents a data-backed story tailored specifically to that customer before the call was placed. This approach resulted in uplifting outbound conversion rate from 13 to 25%.

Calling lists and accompanying scripts were uploaded directly into the centralized calling module, giving agents a single, structured workflow for every outreach. When a call converted, agents could close the sale on the spot or transfer the qualified lead to a field agent in real time — ensuring no warm lead was lost in handoff. This closed-loop engine, from data insight to call to close, eliminated cold calling, improved conversion rates, and created a measurable, repeatable outbound revenue motion.

Unified & Integrated Calling Module

4

Revenue Intelligence & Executive Reporting

Fragmented data sources and manual reporting created blind spots in pipeline performance, limited accountability across the sales force, and left leadership without a reliable, timely view of the business.

Engineered automated data pipelines with CRM and Billing developers, consolidating sales performance data by agent, zone, and product alongside churn and number portability metrics. Built an ETL layer using SQL and Power Query, then developed an interactive reporting suite in Excel and Power BI to surface trends and performance stories clearly.

Designed a monthly Revenue Intelligence Pack for C-suite consumption, consolidating KPIs across revenue, churn, NPS, workforce utilization, and pipeline into a single, visually consistent deck. Eliminated manual report preparation and established a single source of truth for strategic decision-making across the entire SMB commercial operation.

Unified view into product performance

Together, these five initiatives transformed a fragmented, reactive sales operation into a structured, data-driven commercial engine — shortening the order cycle from 9 days to 3, lifting retail-generated leads by 15%, and improving CSAT from 67% to 84%. Every intervention targeted a specific L2C bottleneck, and the cumulative effect was a pipeline that was faster, more predictable, and measurably more profitable.
