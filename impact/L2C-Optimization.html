Lead-to-Cash Optimization

Quote-to-Cash Automation Compressing Deal Velocity Order Accuracy Quote-to-Activation

Ooredoo Qatar's SMB sales operation spans 100+ indirect channel partner sales agents, digital channels, and an outbound telemarketing team — each operating as a largely independent motion with limited data sharing, inconsistent tooling, and fragmented order workflows.

The Lead-to-Cash pipeline had multiple friction points suppressing revenue and inflating costs: leads were identified reactively, agents lacked the data and tools to engage effectively, orders moved through manual paper-based processes, and leadership had limited visibility into performance. I led four concurrent initiatives to systematically eliminate each bottleneck — from first customer contact through to cash collection and executive reporting.

Unified GTM Positioning — From Product-Centric to Customer-Centric

The SMB portfolio was organized by technology type — Mobile, Fixed, ICT, Cloud — with marketing messaging that spoke the language of products, not customers. Reps led with features, customers tuned out, and conversion at the top of funnel was suppressed because the pitch never translated technology into business outcomes.

Authored a unified marketing positioning for the SMB portfolio — rewriting the GTM narrative around six customer-business needs (e.g., "Run my business," "Reach my customers," "Protect my data") rather than technology stacks. Validated the new positioning through customer beta testing, then built the messaging frameworks, talk tracks, and battle cards that translated every product into the language of business outcomes.

The result was a single, customer-business-centric narrative that reps and customers both understood — closing the gap between how Ooredoo described its portfolio and how SMB owners actually thought about their problems.


The unified positioning became the standard messaging across all channels — field sales, telemarketing scripts, marketing campaigns, partner enablement, and the B2B self-service app — ensuring every customer touchpoint told the same outcome-led story regardless of where the conversation started.

2

Order Digitization & Cycle Time Reduction

Non-standardized, incomplete paper order forms were causing downstream operational errors, fulfillment delays, and a poor customer experience — with the order signing cycle routinely stretching to more than 48 hours.

Partnered with Product, Operations, and Marketing UX/UI teams to redesign and standardize all order forms into clean, up-to-date fillable PDF documents and digital forms. Centralized them in the sales repository for online completion, download, or direct sharing with customers. Established a digital signing workflow enabling authorized customer signatories to execute orders remotely — compressing the signing step from 48 hours to under one hour. This was the critical unlock in a broader process redesign that cut the total order-to-activation cycle from 9 days to 3.

3

Data-Driven Outbound Engine

The call center was operating reactively — agents had no targeted prospect lists, no structured scripts, and no mechanism to hand off warm leads to field teams. Outreach was cold, conversion was low, and qualified opportunities were routinely lost.

Mined the customer database to identify high-propensity segments based on usage patterns, tenure, and product heat-map coverage gaps. Generated targeted outbound calling lists enriched with account context, giving calling agents a data-backed story tailored specifically to that customer before the call was placed. This approach resulted in uplifting outbound conversion rate from 18 to 25%.

Calling lists and accompanying scripts were uploaded directly into the centralized calling module, giving agents a single, structured workflow for every outreach. When a call converted, agents could close the sale on the spot or transfer the qualified lead to a field agent in real time — ensuring no warm lead was lost in handoff. This closed-loop engine, from data insight to call to close, eliminated cold calling, improved conversion rates, and created a measurable, repeatable outbound revenue motion.

4

Revenue Intelligence & Executive Reporting

Fragmented data sources and manual reporting created blind spots in pipeline performance, limited accountability across the sales force, and left leadership without a reliable, timely view of the business.

Engineered an executive-grade reporting layer on top of CRM, Order Management, Provisioning, and Billing — consolidating product-line revenue and ARPA, onboarding cycle time and order accuracy, activation success rate, churn, retention, NRR, and number portability into a unified organizational data model with SQL/Power BI. The lens here was the health of the business — which products are growing, which are leaking, where onboarding friction is suppressing realization, and where retention investment is paying back.

Designed and authored a monthly Revenue Intelligence Pack for C-suite consumption — a single, visually consistent deck that replaced fragmented manual reports as the single source of truth for SMB strategic decisions. The pack anchored the monthly executive forecast call and fed the annual operating plan refresh, turning organizational data into a leadership instrument rather than an after-the-fact report.

Unified view into product performance

Together, these four initiatives transformed a fragmented, reactive sales operation into a structured, data-driven commercial engine — shortening the order cycle from 9 days to 3, lifting outbound conversion from 18% to 25%, lifting retail-generated leads by 15%, and improving CSAT from 67% to 84%. Every intervention targeted a specific L2C bottleneck, and the cumulative effect was a pipeline that was faster, more predictable, and measurably more profitable.
