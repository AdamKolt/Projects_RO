VOC— Listening as a Discipline

10 Touch-Points Closed-Loop Feedback NPS / CSAT Framework Customer-Driven Decisions Cross-Functional Listening

Designed and stood up Ooredoo’s SMB integrated Voice of Customer (VOC) program — instrumented on Qualtrics across 10 customer touchpoints spanning digital, retail, telemarketing, field sales, channel partners, and customer service, focusing on quantitative metrics (NPS, CSAT, and CES) and governed through a closed-loop discipline that tied every insight to a defined operational action. The program moved customer voice from fragmented anecdote into a structured commercial input that shaped product, channel, and service decisions.

Context
Ooredoo SMB customers were spread across multiple acquisition channels and service touchpoints, but the company lacked a unified mechanism to listen systematically.

Touchpoint × Survey Channel × Frequency

Each touchpoint was matched to the survey channel best suited to its moment in the customer journey and the cognitive load the customer could absorb at that point. Real-time, low-effort channels (in-app, SMS, IVR) were used at high-frequency touchpoints; more reflective channels (email, partner-administered) were reserved for moments where the customer could engage more deeply. The frequency of each was governed to prevent survey fatigue and ensure response quality stayed high.

Program Design

Touchpoint Mapping & Mode Selection: Started by mapping the SMB customer journey end-to-end — every meaningful interaction across acquisition, onboarding, service, expansion, and exit. Matched each touchpoint to a VOC mode based on moment-of-journey (transactional vs. relational), cognitive load (a customer in the middle of an outage can’t absorb a 10-question NPS), and response channel fit (SMS for high-volume real-time, email for more reflective input, in-person for qualitative depth). The result was 14 touchpoints with fit-for-purpose modes, not a one-size-fits-all survey blasted indiscriminately.
Closed-Loop Feedback Discipline: Built the operating mechanism that turned VOC from a measurement exercise into a customer-trust signal. Every detractor (NPS <7) and dissatisfied responder (CSAT <3) triggered an automated case routed to the responsible function, with a 48-hour response SLA. Customers were called back — not surveyed again, but contacted by a human who acknowledged the feedback and either fixed the issue or explained what would happen next. Achieved 92% closed-loop response within 48 hours; closure measurably reduced churn on customers who had given negative feedback.
Cross-Functional Governance & Single Source of Truth: Built a unified VOC dashboard consolidating signals from all 14 touchpoints into one governed view. Established a weekly cross-functional review with Sales, Service, Product, Marketing, and Operations — same data, same definitions, shared accountability. Customer voice stopped being something each function interpreted privately and became a shared commercial input on which decisions were made together.
Anti-Fatigue Design: Capped survey frequency per customer at no more than two touches per monthr for the same service, with cross-channel deduplication so a customer who took an in-shop CSAT wasn’t also pulled into the relational survey that quarter. The discipline preserved response rate quality (sustained ~22% average) and protected the customer experience from being degraded by the feedback program itself.
Frontline Listening Training: Trained sales reps, retail agents, and service staff on active listening as a structured skill — how to surface unspoken concerns, how to ask follow-up questions that produce signal rather than confirmation, how to capture qualitative input in a structured way that fed the VOC system. Frontline staff stopped being passive collectors of survey data and became active sources of customer insight in their own right.
VOC-to-Action Pipeline: Every cross-functional review tied insights to a specific decision or operational change — product roadmap adjustments, channel redesigns, service-process refinements, training updates. Insights without owned actions were treated as unfinished work. Over time, customer voice influenced product backlog prioritization, channel conflict rules, retail staffing models, and service SLAs. VOC stopped being a report and started being a steering input.

Operating Disciplines

Close the loop, or don’t ask: Asking customers for feedback you can’t or won’t act on is worse than not asking. The 48-hour closed-loop SLA was the foundation that made the entire program credible to customers.
Match the mode to the moment: A customer in flow (digital onboarding) needs a one-tap CSAT; a churning customer needs an exit interview; a partner relationship needs a quarterly relational survey. One-size-fits-all surveying produces low response and low signal.
Cap survey frequency aggressively: Survey fatigue is the silent killer of VOC programs. Two touches per quarter per customer was the ceiling that preserved response quality.
Single source of truth: Customer voice fragments naturally across functions. Without a governed unified dashboard with shared definitions, every function interprets the customer differently and the organization makes decisions on partial views.
Layer quantitative and qualitative: Metrics tell you what; conversations tell you why. Either alone is insufficient. The most useful insights came from triangulating between them.
Tie every insight to an owned action: Insights without action become noise. Each cross-functional review ended with named owners and dated commitments — or the insight was deprioritized rather than left in limbo.
Make leadership feel the customer: Dashboards are abstractions. Monthly leadership ride-alongs, advisory board sessions, and in-person customer visits keep senior decision-makers calibrated to the actual customer experience, not just its metrics representation.
Train frontline as listeners, not collectors: The richest signal comes from people already in front of customers. Train them to hear, not just to push surveys, and the qualitative channel becomes a primary intelligence source.
Anonymize where it improves honesty: Customers under-report negative feedback when they think it’s attributable. Strategic anonymization on relational surveys raised honesty; transactional surveys stayed identified to enable closed-loop response.
Outcomes
  • NPS lifted +14 points across the SMB segment over the program’s first year, driven by closed-loop response and operational changes informed by customer voice.
  • 92% of detractor feedback closed within 48 hours through the structured closed-loop mechanism, measurably reducing churn among customers who had given negative feedback.
  • Customer voice influenced product roadmap, channel conflict rules, retail staffing models, and service SLAs — VOC moved from sentiment report to structured commercial input shaping operational decisions.
  • Cross-functional adoption across Sales, Service, Product, Marketing, and Operations — all operating from the same unified VOC dashboard with shared definitions and shared accountability.
  • Frontline listening capability embedded across reps, retail agents, and service staff — converting customer-facing roles into active intelligence sources, not just transactional touchpoints.
  • Sustained 22% average response rate through anti-fatigue design and disciplined frequency capping — preserving signal quality as the program scaled.
