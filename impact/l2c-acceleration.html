GTM Positioning — From Product-Centric to Customer-Centric

The SMB portfolio was organized by technology type — Mobile, Fixed, ICT, Cloud — with marketing messaging that spoke the language of products, not customers. Reps led with features, customers tuned out, and conversion at the top of funnel was suppressed because the pitch never translated technology into business outcomes.

Authored a unified marketing positioning for the SMB portfolio — rewriting the GTM narrative around six customer-business needs (e.g., "Run my business," "Reach my customers," "Protect my data") rather than technology stacks. Validated the new positioning through customer beta testing, then built the messaging frameworks, talk tracks, and battle cards that translated every product into the language of business outcomes.

The result was a single, customer-business-centric narrative that reps and customers both understood — closing the gap between how Ooredoo described its portfolio and how SMB owners actually thought about their problems.


The unified positioning became the standard messaging across all channels — field sales, telemarketing scripts, marketing campaigns, business website, and the B2B self-service app — ensuring every customer touchpoint told the same outcome-led story regardless of where the conversation started.

Redesigned Order-to-Cash Workflow — Quote-to-Sign Compression

Non-standardized, dated, and incomplete paper order forms were causing downstream operational errors, fulfillment delays, revenue leakage, and a poor customer experience — with the order signing cycle routinely stretching to more than 48 hours.

Architected the order digitization workstream across Product, Operations, and Marketing UX/UI — redesigning every SMB order form into a structured digital experience with guided fields, built-in validation, and conditional logic that surfaced only the relevant options for the chosen product and bundle. Integrated e-signature with submissions flowing directly into CRM and Order Management — eliminating paper handling and re-keying entirely.

Embedded forms into the PLM process so every product launch reached the field with order-ready forms validated, signed-off, and CRM-routable from day one — no more launch-day improvisation. The remote digital signing workflow compressed quote-to-sign from 48 hours to under 4 hours, the critical unlock in a broader process redesign that cut total order-to-activation from 9 days to 3 — eliminating manual rework, reducing revenue leakage, and accelerating revenue recognition.

Redesigned the Outbound Revenue Engine

The Inside Sales team was operating reactively with no targeted prospect lists, no structured scripts, and no automated mechanism to hand off warm leads to field teams. Outreach was cold, conversion was low, and qualified opportunities were routinely lost — a measurable revenue leakage point.

Redesigned the outbound commercial workflow end-to-end. Mined the customer database to identify high-propensity segments based on usage patterns, tenure, and product heat-map coverage gaps, then generated targeted outbound calling lists enriched with account context — giving agents a data-backed story tailored to each customer before the call was placed. Outbound conversion lifted from 18% to 25%.

Standardized the outbound workflow inside a unified calling module — calling lists, scripts, and objection libraries in one structured surface. When a call converted, agents could close on the spot or hand the qualified lead off to a field rep in real time. Every field rep was equipped with a mobile field app that alerted them the moment a lead landed in their zone — with full account context and talk track. The result: quote-to-sign on field-routed leads compressed from 48 hours to 4 hours, eliminating handoff loss and accelerating revenue recognition. The closed-loop engine turned cold calling into a measurable, repeatable outbound revenue motion with attribution back to the lead source.

Cross-Channel Lead Routing — Retail-to-Field Handoff via Digital Catalog

Retail shops engaged thousands of SMB owners every week, but had no commercial path for high-intent customers whose deals exceeded retail’s scope or required field follow-up. Leads stalled at the counter, were captured on paper, or never made it into CRM.

Architected a cross-channel routing motion that connected retail shops to the field sales reps through the digital product catalog. Shop agents engaged customers inside the catalog — features, pricing, and bundles — and when the customer signaled intent, the shop agent routes a qualified lead to the field rep assigned to the customer's zone, with full account context and shop-agent attribution preserved.

Standardized the handoff protocol with dual-credit attribution rules (originating shop agent + closing field rep) and CRM stage gates that prevented leads from going dark in transit. Retail became a qualified lead-generation engine for the field motion rather than a walled-off transaction channel — lifting retail-generated leads by 15% and giving the field rep a warm, context-rich handoff instead of a cold callback.

Implemented Executive KPI Operating Cadence — Forecast Confidence

Fragmented data sources and manual reporting created blind spots in pipeline performance, suppressed forecast confidence, and left leadership without a reliable, timely view of the business — making capital and capacity decisions slow and reactive.

Implemented an executive KPI operating cadence on top of CRM, Order Management, Provisioning, and Billing — consolidating product-line revenue, ARPU trends, onboarding new logos, activations, churn, retention, NRR, and number portability into a unified organizational data model.

Designed and authored a monthly Revenue Intelligence Pack for C-suite consumption — a single, visually consistent deck that replaced fragmented manual reports as the single source of truth for SMB strategic decisions. The pack anchored the monthly executive forecast call and fed the annual operating plan refresh, lifting forecast accuracy to 95% with 3.3× pipeline coverage — turning organizational data into a leadership instrument rather than an after-the-fact report.
