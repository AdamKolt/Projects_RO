Strategic Alliances

Hyperscaler Co-Sell VAS Partnerships SaaS Attach Motion M365 Doubled Two Years Running Multi-Vendor Stack

Beyond direct and reseller distribution, I built Ooredoo's hyperscaler and OEM co-sell motion — a library of structured Sales Plays that diversified the SMB revenue mix from telecom-only services into a multi-vendor value-added services (VAS) portfolio. Each Play paired a target customer cohort, value hypothesis, talk track, content kit, seller incentives, and measurable outcome — deployed across the SMB base concurrently, with a quarterly review and refresh cadence to keep each Play current with product, market, and competitive shifts.

Context
The SMB telecom market in Qatar was approaching saturation, with price-driven competition compressing margins on core connectivity. Ooredoo needed a structural answer: more revenue per account, not more accounts at lower prices.
Proposition
Partner with hyperscalers and OEMs to co-sell productivity SaaS, cloud infrastructure, and managed security as branded Ooredoo offerings — turning the SMB telecom contract into a multi-product digital relationship.

Microsoft 365 Attach Sales Play: Built and ran a structured Sales Play with Microsoft — target cohort (existing customers without M365, plus customers using competing productivity suites), value hypothesis, talk track, co-branded battlecards, attach scripts, dedicated channel comp incentives, and seller certification — that doubled M365 seats two years running across the SMB base. The Play turned every Ooredoo telecom contract into a productivity-suite conversation.
Recurring SaaS Portfolio Expansion: Expanded the co-sell catalog beyond Microsoft 365 to include Google Workspace, Copilot, NotebookLM, and other productivity SaaS — converting one-time telecom transactions into multi-year recurring SaaS relationships that strengthened NRR.
IaaS & Cloud Compute Co-Sell: Stood up cloud compute (IaaS) co-sell motions with hyperscaler partners — enabling SMB customers to consume cloud infrastructure through their Ooredoo relationship and bill, eliminating procurement friction and accelerating digital adoption.
Hosted Security Services: Packaged cybersecurity into the SMB commercial bundle through partnerships for Microsoft Defender, DDoS protection, and managed firewall services — making Ooredoo the one-stop digital security partner for small and mid-sized businesses.
Managed Networking & Smart-Site Stack: Sold and ran GTM for a multi-vendor managed networking and security stack — SDN, Cisco Meraki, Huawei Smart Wi-Fi, managed routers, CCTV-as-a-Service, Web Application Firewalls (WAF), Next-Generation Firewalls (NGFW), Connected Point of Sale (POS) terminals, smart boards, and bespoke smart home solutions. The motion turned Ooredoo into the SMB customer's single managed-services partner across connectivity, networking, and physical/cyber security.
Joint GTM & Sales Enablement: Co-developed sales enablement content with hyperscaler partners — battle cards, attach scripts, competitive teardowns, and certification modules — produced through the AI-native content factory (CoPilot, Claude) so each Play could be scaled across segments and refreshed at velocity. The result: SMB sales force gained product-specific fluency across the VAS portfolio without expanding headcount.
Outcome: The VAS portfolio lifted ARPA 5% and LTV 4%, and contributed to NRR reaching 104% in a saturated, price-pressured telecom market — proving that channel partnerships could outpace pricing as a growth lever.
Outcomes
  • Diversified the SMB revenue mix from telecom-only to multi-vendor VAS portfolio
  • Doubled M365 seats two years running through a structured attach Sales Play
  • Lifted ARPA 5% and LTV 4% via VAS attach
  • Sustained NRR at 104% in a saturated, price-pressured market
  • Built a partnership-led growth engine that outpaced pricing as the primary lever
