B2B2C — Embedded Distribution

Conceived and delivered a tri-party telecom platform enabling residential complex operators to activate and manage home fiber broadband for their tenants at preferential rates — growing Ooredoo's residential fiber market share and protecting existing base.

Ooredoo Qatar is the incumbent telecom operator defending against the challenger Vodafone Qatar over market share in the mobile and fixed consumer segments.
Problem: As the incumbent operator, Ooredoo Qatar faced pressure of aggressive discounting in residential fixed broadband segment, resulting in declining market share in communities where tenants made connectivity decisions independently at move-in, without incentive to favor Ooredoo.
Proposition: Partner with property managers to embed Ooredoo into the leasing process, design a tri-party framework that gives landlords a revenue-generating tool, tenants an effortless move-in experience, and Ooredoo a zero-cost customer acquisition channel.
Landlords
Access to bulk-rate services to create competitive rental offerings. A self-service digital tool to activate or deactivate services, eliminating uncompensated utility costs.
Tenants
Immediate, hassle-free connectivity at move-in at discounted rates. Full flexibility to upgrade with personal add-ons, speed boosts, TV channel via the Ooredoo consumer app.
Ooredoo
Exclusive long-term contracts effectively blocking competitor entry and securing a zero-cost customer acquisition channel with predictable long-term revenue.
GTM Channel Architecture: Identified the precise points in the leasing workflow where Ooredoo could be embedded to create a zero-cost acquisition trigger — designing a tri-party integration that made connectivity activation a natural part of tenant move-in rather than an independent post-move purchasing decision. Front-end simplicity was paramount; all technical complexity was abstracted through back-end API integration.
Split-Billing Architecture: Architected a hybrid billing model with CRM, Billing, and IT teams that cleanly separated base charges to the landlord from tenant-level charges. This preserved Ooredoo's competitive advantage while ensuring partner profitability — and integrated fully with existing BSS/OSS systems with no disruption to desktop operations.
Consumer App Integration & Upsell Channel: Defined and drove requirements to extend the platform to B2B2C-activated subscribers — enabling self-service plan management, usage visibility, bill payment, and support. Once activated through the landlord, tenants could independently upgrade to higher speeds, TV channels, and add-ons through the Ooredoo app, creating a self-serve upsell channel with no incremental sales cost.
Go-to-Market Execution: Developed co-branded partner playbooks, commercial term sheets, and onboarding kits. Designed and implemented a dedicated account management model to sustain and grow each B2B partner relationship, with a structured training programme for the B2B sales team ahead of launch.
Outcome: The platform achieved rapid adoption — 100+ property operators onboarded and 500K+ subscribers brought within the B2B2C framework. Within the first year, Ooredoo's residential fiber market share in the target segment grew by 2 percentage points — a meaningful result in a competitive, price-sensitive market delivered through structural channel advantage rather than margin erosion.

SOHO Indirect Channel — Partner-Led Distribution

Recruited and enabled 5 boutique partner firms with ~125 frontline agents to deliver Sales and Retention to the SOHO segment of Ooredoo's SMB business — equipped with the same tools, content, and certification as direct sellers, so partners could acquire, onboard, and retain customers autonomously without degrading customer experience.

The SOHO segment (single-owner, sub-10-employee businesses) was too granular and geographically dispersed for direct field reps to cover economically — but too high-volume and high-touch to leave to digital alone. A boutique-partner channel was the only way to acquire and retain at scale without inflating CAC.
Proposition: Recruit specialist boutique sales firms with established SOHO relationships, equip them with the same enablement stack as direct reps, and design commercial terms that align partner incentives with Ooredoo's NRR and ARPA goals — not just acquisition volume.
Partner Recruiting & Selection: Identified and recruited 5 boutique sales firms with proven SOHO segment expertise and established customer relationships. Negotiated tiered commercial agreements aligning partner economics with Ooredoo's growth and retention targets, not just acquisition volume.
Partner Organizational Design & Shop Integration: Worked with each partner firm to design their internal operating structure — establishing dedicated units for telemarketing, back-office support, and field sales, with team-level roles (sales coaches, team leads, reps) mirroring Ooredoo's own organization. For partners running their own retail shops, equipped shop staff to sell on the same stack — with secure CRM connectivity and full integration into Ooredoo's centralized lead-capture flow, so partner-shop walk-ins entered the same pipeline as every other channel.
Partner Quota Engineering & Cascade: As part of carrying the SMB division quota, engineered each partner firm's annual quota based on territory potential, agent count, and product mix — then guided partner leadership on how to cascade it to quarterly targets, weekly run-rates, and per-agent commitments. Provided the templates and dashboards partners used to track against the cascade, giving Ooredoo agent-level forecasting resolution within the indirect channel.
Connected-Tablet Stack for Partners: Equipped every partner agent with the same rugged, LTE-connected tablet stack as direct field reps — full mobile CRM, the centralized CMS with current pricing and battle cards, and digital order forms with e-signature. Partners operated from the same single source of truth as Ooredoo's own sellers.
Co-Sell Playbooks & Commercial Bundles: Built role-specific co-sell playbooks (discovery scripts, objection libraries, competitive positioning) and pre-configured commercial bundles tuned to SOHO buying patterns — so partner agents could quote and close without escalation, without giving up margin or unit economics.
Academy Access & Tier Certification: Extended the B2B Sales Academy to partner agents — they completed the same Sales Associate / Sales Expert certification path as internal sellers. Certification gated access to complex deals and tier-2 commercial terms, ensuring partners selling on Ooredoo's behalf were trained to Ooredoo's standard.
Sales + Retention Independence: Partners delivered both new acquisition and base retention — handling escalations, plan upgrades, and renewal conversations directly. Ooredoo provided supervisory tooling and performance dashboards; partners ran the day-to-day. Customer experience held steady, and retention save-ratio matched direct rep benchmarks.
Outcome: The SOHO indirect channel program scaled distribution into a previously uneconomical segment, contributed to 5% YoY revenue growth and NRR reaching 104%, and protected the SMB base against aggressive challenger pricing — all without proportional direct-sales headcount.
